THE PROBLEM

As technology progresses, we are using ever more digital tools and services. Statistic from SBB show that internet usage from the Norwegian population has increased from 7%[9] to 96%[10] in just 20 years. As a result of this, we leave behind bigger and more substantial amounts of personal data at the different providers of digital services. As well as at companies which are based on collection and selling of data. Some of which is information we freely hand out, but a large portion of this information that is obtained, is assembled without the knowledge of the user.

Statistics from SBB’s yearly examination about Norwegians Media habits substantiates the claim that we in an increasing degree live “online”.

Collection of information: The collection of user data accurse in many different forms/processes. Some of them more apparent than others. In todays society most of them are known for taking in huge sizes of data through the use of social Media platforms. But how many individuals have an overview over which authorizations we allow these Service providers to posses, by accepting the terms and conditions of these Services.

Therefore the average user isn’t aware of other data, that gets collected via:

  • Browser-trackers
  • Browser fingerprints
  • Location Services
  • Smartphone applications
  • "Smart"-gadgets with connectivity (IoT)

Our vision is thereby:

We wish to establish a larger awareness over collection of data and how these are passed down. The main challenge to achieve this lies within that the average user has difficulties to get an overview over the amount of digital footprints that are left behind via browsers, smartphones and other connected units.

Our solution to this problem is called “ShareSafe” which you can read about by selecting the solution page in the navigation bar or read more here.
